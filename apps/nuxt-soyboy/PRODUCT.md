# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People curious about or actively practicing veganism — home cooks, people newly exploring plant-based eating, and people who've been told vegan food is boring or that vegans lack strength. They come to browse recipes and want immediate clarity: ingredients, method, quantities. They should feel welcome, not lectured at.

## Product Purpose

Soyboy is Saajan Patel's personal vegan recipe site and a form of activism. The goal is to dismantle two stigmas: that vegan food is bland and lacks character, and that vegans are weak or soft. The site does this not through argument but through the food itself and through the site's personality. Recipes are the primary content. Ease of access when viewing a recipe — ingredients, steps, quantities — is a core product requirement. Success looks like someone trying a recipe and feeling like veganism is modern, exciting, and completely accessible to them.

## Brand Personality

Charismatic, welcoming, unapologetic.

The site has a distinct point of view and isn't shy about it. It challenges stereotypes without being preachy. It draws people in rather than pushing an agenda at them. It's confident, not aggressive.

## Anti-references

- BBC Good Food and similar blocky, WordPress-style recipe aggregators — heavy, corporate, zero personality, template-grade layouts
- Clinical health-food brands — sterile whites, green wellness aesthetic, detox-program vibes
- Hippie farmer's market aesthetics — earthy browns, handwritten fonts, rustic textures, soft and apologetic
- Generic vegan activist sites — heavy on the lecturing, light on the delight

## Design Principles

1. **The site has a point of view.** Every design decision should feel intentional and characterful, not like a template someone filled in. If it could belong to any recipe site, it belongs to none.
2. **Challenge by showing, not telling.** Don't explain that vegan food is bold and exciting — demonstrate it through the visual energy of the site. The design is the argument.
3. **Modern without being cold.** Slick and contemporary, but warm enough that a first-time visitor feels welcome rather than intimidated.
4. **Recipes are the product.** On any recipe page, the ingredients and method must be immediately legible and easy to navigate. No friction between the user and the food.
5. **Confidence is welcoming.** Unapologetic design isn't exclusive — it's inviting. The site's boldness should feel like a personality, not a barrier.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA standard. No specific known user needs beyond that. High contrast is already a strength of the existing dark palette — maintain it.
